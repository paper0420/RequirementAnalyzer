  • 6.1.26.1 Description
  • 6.1.26.1.0-1 Brief description:
  • 6.1.26.1.0-2 The LVSupplySystem transfers energy when the target voltage is higher than the measured voltage. Between 14V and 15,5V the limiting factor shall be the given limits from HVPM and the cooling.
  • 6.1.26.1.0-3 Preconditions:

  • 6.1.26.1.0-6 Target_Operating_State = Buck, Target_U_LV = 15V, The electronic DC load power is adjusted to 3100 W The electronic DC load voltage is adjusted to 15V. The regulation mode of the electronic DC load shall be constant power.
  • 6.1.26.1.0-7 Input data:
  • 6.1.26.1.0-8 Target_Operating_State = Buck, Target_U_LV = 15V, Measured_U_LV = 14,9V and 14V Measured_U_HV = 380V Min_Permitted_HV_Voltage = 300V, Max_Permitted_HV_Power (Leistung_DCDC-Wandler_Hochvoltteil_maximal_Grenze) = 3800 W, p_DCDC_CONTINUOUS_I_LV = 243 A, p_DCDC_CONTINUOUS_P_LV = 3400 W, Coolant temperature = 45째C
  • 6.1.26.1.0-9 Description of behaviour:
  • 6.1.26.1.0-10 This use case shall test the functional buck mode behaviour without any intension to check the maximum deliverable power. The LV voltage range 14V to 15V and the maximum power of 3400W is choosen to have a current output <= 243A. The input current from HV must be calculated higher due to the efficiency losses. For example 10% efficiency losses is expected. The difference between target value and current voltage value and the connected power load determines the level of transported power into the LV bordnet. The usecase shall test, if the LvDCDC is able to deliver the power. The cooling shall be always sufficient to keep the cooling plates to max. 45째C.
