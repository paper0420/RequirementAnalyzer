SYR_LvDCDC_F_380


  • 6.1.51 SYR_LvDCDC_F_380: No leave of the buck mode during undervoltage of KL_30 or 12V onboard supply voltage between 6V and 4,5V for not longer than 500ms.
  • 6.1.51.1 Description
  • 6.1.51.1.0-1 Brief description:
  • 6.1.51.1.0-2 The LvDCDC does not leave the buck mode during undervoltage of KL_30 or LV voltage between 6V and 4,5V < 500ms. The energy transfer may pause. When the undervoltage situation is finished, the energy transfers resumes within 1ms.
  • 6.1.51.1.0-3 Preconditions:
  • 6.1.51.1.0-4 LvDCDC current operating state=Buck. RUNLEVEL >= 30,
  • 6.1.51.1.0-5 Trigger:
  • 6.1.51.1.0-6 Measured_U_LV = 4,5V, duration = 496ms
  • 6.1.51.1.0-7 Input data:
  • 6.1.51.1.0-8 Target_Operating_State = Buck, Target_U_LV = 14V, Measured_U_LV = 12V, 9V (10s), 4,5V (496ms), 9V (1s), 12V Measured_U_HV = e.g. 380V, Min_Permitted_HV_Voltage = 300V, Max_Permitted_HV_Power = 4000 W, p_DCDC_CONTINUOUS_I_LV = e.g. 221 A, p_DCDC_CONTINUOUS_P_LV = e.g. 3100 W, Coolant temperature = e.g. 35Â°C
  • 6.1.51.1.0-9 Description of behaviour:
  • 6.1.51.1.0-10 The energy transfer shall be zero without changing the operating state when the LV voltage (KL_30 or 12V onbord supply) drops down between 6V and 4,5V for not more than 500ms. [Requirement 23420]

  • 6.1.51.1.0-17 If the LvDCDC reacts on the undervoltage at the LV+ to LV- without any debouncing time, then the LvDCDC will go into the error mode earlier than within the requested delay time of 500ms.
