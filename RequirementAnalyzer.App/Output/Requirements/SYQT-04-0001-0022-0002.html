  • 18838 -
    [Not Changed][accepted][SYQT (ENG.10)][Information]
    The pilot signal is generated by the EVSE (see figure: Pilot as per IEC and SAE). If the charging plug is not plugged, the pilot signal is in the voltage range A1/A2 (from 10.7 V to 13.3 V). If the charging plug is plugged into a vehicle, the pilot signal falls to the voltage range B1/B2 (from 7.7 V to 10.3 V) and the EVSE can detect the vehicle. If the vehicle is detected, the EVSE starts a PWM signal with transmission on the pilot line of the maximum current limit of the EVSE. If the vehicle signals "Ladebereit" due to closing of switch "S2", the pilot signal falls to the voltage range C1/C2 (from 4.7 V to 7.3 V) and the EVSE connects through the AC grid current.

    [Not Changed][partly accepted][SYQT (ENG.10)HWT (ENG.7/8)Review][Requirement]
